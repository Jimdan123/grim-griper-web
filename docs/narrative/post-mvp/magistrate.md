# Magistrate's Hall

## Setting

A stone hall with a raised bench at one end, a defendant's iron-railed dock at the other, and a tall window showing the gallows-hill in the distance. Heavy ledgers chained to the bench. A branding iron resting in the hearth — used to mark the condemned.

The Lord lives in private chambers off the hall; in MVP terms, *this hall is his entire world*. He sleeps in the side-room and works here.

## Victim — Lord Vesry, Magistrate

Tall, lean, late fifties. Powerful and used to it. Sells verdicts to the highest noble bidder — condemns innocents (usually peasants) for crimes he invents, then seizes their property "as forfeit". When threatened, he does not flee or call for help — he **lashes out**, demanding the intruder be arrested, threatening invisible guards. His authority is the only weapon he's ever needed.

**Personality bias:** `wrongWaypointReactsAs: "AGGRESSIVE"`, `probability: 0.65`. Else → haunt's primary tendency.

## Tone

Procedural horror. Vesry is bored by his own cruelty — it's just paperwork. The Reaper investigates a man who never lost a night's sleep.

## Visual hooks

- Window light should be the visual anchor — cold gallows-hill view dominates the middle of the canvas.
- Branding iron in the hearth glows red even outside Reaper Sight; only under Sight does the ghost replay reveal who it was used on.
