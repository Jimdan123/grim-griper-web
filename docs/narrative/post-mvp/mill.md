# Mill on the Bog

## Setting

A timber mill perched on a sluice over a black bog. Inside, the millstone grinds slowly. Sacks of "flour" stacked against the walls — some labeled, some not. Outside the door, the bog water laps at the wooden supports; in the ghost replays, *something pale floats just under the surface*.

Famine has driven flour prices skyward. Rauk has discovered that bone — fresh bone, dried then ground — passes for flour to nobles who never grew up eating it. He sources the bone from the bog, where pauper-graves of plague victims are barely covered.

## Victim — Goodman Rauk, Miller

Heavy, ruddy, peasant stock. Late forties. No education, no allies — only the mill, which his father left him. He knows what he's doing is a sin; he does it because his children are hungry too. When threatened, he **runs** — he's a peasant. He runs from everything bigger than himself, and almost everything is bigger than him.

**Personality bias:** `wrongWaypointReactsAs: "FLEEING"`, `probability: 0.75`. Else → haunt's primary tendency.

## Tone

Peasant horror. Rauk is the only victim in the roster who *might* deserve sympathy — which is the point. The Reaper does not weigh that; the debt is the debt. The player feels the moral weight; the Reaper does not.

## Visual hooks

- The mill is **vertical** — millstone, hopper, sluice gate, ceiling beams. Different silhouette from the chapel.
- Outside the window, the bog should be a flat near-black band along the lower third of the canvas. In ghost replays, faint pale shapes drift in it.
- The millstone turns *slowly* even in the present; the only constant motion in the room.
